Irina Proskurina

PhD student working on language model compression

I am a PhD student at the ERIC Lab, a research unit affiliated with the University of Lyon and Lyon 2, under the supervision of Prof. Julien Velcin and Assoc. Prof. Guillaume Metzler. My research focuses on estimating and predicting the behavior of compressed language models, with particular attention to biased outcomes resulting from compression. Additionally, I am working on developing new French benchmarks that could help mitigate these biases. The website of the ANR project, which my PhD is part of, is accessible here.

Prior to my PhD, I obtained my MSc from the Higher School of Economics University (HSE) and completed the second year of my master’s in the picturesque city of Clermont-Ferrand, located in the central part of France, as part of a double degree program. During my master studies, I worked as a research intern at the Learner Corpora Lab (HSE), where I collaborated with a team of corpus and computational linguists on grammatical error detection in learner-written essays and worked with the JetBrains team to enhance the Grazie tool. I also worked at the Laboratory for Models and Methods of Computational Pragmatics at HSE on the application of topological data analysis to grammatical acceptability judgments.

When Quantization Affects Confidence of Large Language Models?
NAACL 2024 June 2024
Irina Proskurina Luc Brun Guillaume Metzler Julien Velcin

We investigate the confidence and calibration of quantized models and show that 4-bit quantization with GPTQ decreases confidence in true labels, with varying impacts across different language models and scales.

Mini minds: Exploring Bebeshka and Zlata baby models
BabyLM @ CoNLL 2023 Dec 2023
Irina Proskurina Guillaume Metzler Julien Velcin

We describe the University of Lyon 2 submission to the STRICT-SMALL track of the BabyLM competition. We introduce two small-size language models, a 4-layer encoder with 8 attention heads and a 6-layer decoder model with 12 heads which we term Bebeshka and Zlata. Despite being half the scale of the baseline LMs, our proposed models achieve comparable performance. We further explore the applicability of small-scale language models in tasks involving moral judgments.

The Other Side of Compression: Measuring Bias in Pruned Transformers
IDA 2023 April 2023
Irina Proskurina Guillaume Metzler Julien Velcin

We evaluate bias in compressed models fine-tuned on hate speech data and estimate to which extent the pruned models capture the relevant context when classifying the input text as hateful, offensive or neutral. Results of our experiments show that transformer-based encoders with 70% or fewer preserved weights are prone to gender, racial, and religious identity-based bias, even if the performance loss is insignificant.

Can BERT eat RuCoLA? Topological Data Analysis to Explain
SlavicNLP @ EACL 2023 May 2023
Irina Proskurina Ekaterina Artemova Irina Piontkovskaya

We investigate how Transformer language models fine-tuned for acceptability classification capture linguistic features. We introduce novel topological features, chordality, and the matching number, and show that TDA-based classifiers outperform fine-tuning baselines. We propose several black-box introspection techniques aimed at detecting changes in the attention mode of the LMs during fine-tuning, defining the LM’s prediction confidences, and associating individual heads with fine-grained grammar phenomena.
